Al momento de redactar este documento, Chile y el mundo 
buscan frenéticamente encontrar respuestas a inmensos 
desafíos que afectan de manera crucial a la humanidad 
y a toda forma de vida en el planeta. Tanto así, que los 
modelos de crecimiento económico y social establecidos 
parecen estar perdiendo legitimidad, cuestionándose 
los equilibrios y acuerdos previamente alcanzados e 
impulsándonos, a pasos acelerados, a hacernos cargo 
de diseñar activamente nuestro futuro.
Afortunadamente, la irrupción de tecnologías computa -
cionales de alto desempeño, distribuidas y en la nube, 
junto a la creciente capacidad de captura, procesamiento 
y análisis de datos, nos permiten hoy estudiar virtual -
mente múltiples escenarios y prototipar sus posibles 
soluciones, proveyéndonos así de mejores respuestas 
(¡o quizás de más preguntas!). Así, la digitalización en sí 
constituye la piedra angular para conceptualizar, diseñar, 
implementar y operar cualquier sistema inteligente con 
miras a garantizar su propia sostenibilidad.
Todo lo anterior hace extremadamente complejo, y 
al mismo tiempo fascinante, el ejercicio de imaginar 
estrategias que conlleven a nuevos y mejores modelos 
de convivencia social. Para esto necesitaremos generar 
oportunidades respetando nuestro entorno enmarcados 
en un contexto global; en un mundo que es, al mismo 
tiempo, diverso, incierto y dinámico. Un mejor aprove -
chamiento de nuestros recursos humanos y naturales, 
sin comprometer aquellos que futuras generaciones 
necesitarán, requiere de un pensamiento interdisciplinario, 
sistémico y vanguardista. → 01

nuestra Facultad asume con optimismo y responsabili -

Decano_ At the time of writing this document, Chile and the World 
frantically seek to ﬁnd answers to immense challenges 
that crucially aﬀect humanity and all life on the planet. 
So much so, that the established economic and social 
growth models seem to be losing legitimacy, questioning 
the equilibriums and agreements previously reached and 
driving us, at accelerated steps, to take charge of actively 
designing our future.
Fortunately, the emergence of high-performance, distributed 
and cloud computing technologies, along with increasing 
capacity for data collection, processing and analysis, 
today allow us to virtually study multiple scenarios 
and prototype their possible solutions, thus providing 
us with better responses (or maybe more questions!). 
Digitalization itself is therefore the cornerstone for the 
conceptualization, design, implementation and operation 
of any smart system with a view to guaranteeing its 
own sustainability.
All of the above makes the exercise of imagining strategies 
that lead to new and better models of social coexistence 
extremely complex and fascinating at the same time. To 
do this, we will need to generate opportunities that respect 
our environment and are framed in a global context; in 
a world that is also diverse, uncertain and dynamic. A 
better use of our human and natural resources, without 
compromising those that future generations will need, 
requires interdisciplinary, systemic and avant-garde thinking.From this perspective, and with a view towards 2030, our 
faculty assumes the challenge of actively contributing to 
sustainable development with optimism and responsibility. 
Inspired by this purpose, and understanding that establishing 
a digital culture that incorporates Data Science into all of 
our work is essential to achieving the desired sustainability, 
we have synthesized our strategic plan for the next ﬁve 
years in the Smart+Sustainable formula.
On the following pages we present how this formula will 
set the direction we will give to our undergraduate and 
graduate training; how it will inﬂuence the impact and 
scope of our research; how it will permeate the transfer of 
knowledge and innovation, and ﬁnally, how it will allow 
us to catalyze changes in our ecosystems.
We therefore invite our entire community to implement 
and carry out this Strategic Plan, the result of a collaborative 
exercise, so that together we can reach the ambitious goals 
that we have set for ourselves. All contributions, points 
of view and talents, from the smallest to the largest, will 
allow us to build a sustainable world.“NECESITAREMOS GENERAR

REACCIONAR NO ES OPCIÓN._ In this context, new technologies play a leading role: 
Engineering and Science have the opportunity to lead 
the major transformations and solutions to challenges 
that are becoming increasingly complex and critical. How 
can we not be inspired by the endless opportunities that 
scientiﬁc and technological advances open up and by 
the ways these can be integrated into our society? How 
can we not be curious about those possibilities not even 
imagined, but waiting to be addressed?
These changes create uncertainty regarding their impact: 
Will scientiﬁc and technological advances bring about 
the expected well-being? The truth is that the future is 
a moving target and is determined by how we imagine 
it and by the actions we undertake to move forward in 
a  particular direction. How can we shift towards green 
mining? How do we deal with the eﬃcient management of 
our water resources to face the water crisis and the global 
drought situation? What is required to ensure that food 
production is carried out in a sustainable way? How far 
is it possible to advance in the creation of new materials that allow us to radically modify technologies? The future 
is here; engineering and science give us the freedom to 
choose the direction we want to take.
Addressing the challenges of our society, embodied in 
the set of Sustainable Development Goals of the United 
Nations, is the task that has defined the roadmap of 
our faculty in the coming years. Only in this way can we 
contribute to the well-being of our planet and positively 
impact future generations with our work. In academia, we 
can contribute to these goals through excellence in the 
training of engineers who can count on the tools and skills 
required to adapt to these fast changes in a creative and 
timely way. We are committed to driving the generation 
of new knowledge that enables us to move decisively into 
the future we have envisioned. We require the diversity of 
the ideas, skills and experiences of our entire community 
to add social and economic value to our country, and to 
contribute to building a sustainable world through the 
development of innovative solutions._ Society today faces dizzying changes that force us to be alert

_ MINA PLANTA CHANCÓN SCHOOL
La Facultad de Ingeniería y Ciencias dispone de un espacio natural y técnicamente adecuado para que 
estudiantes que cursan carreras mineras puedan realizar sus prácticas de formación y cuenten con las 
herramientas necesarias para desarrollarse profesionalmente. La Escuela Mina Planta está ubicada en 
el distrito minero de Chancón, Región del Libertador Bernardo O´Higgins, y es un proyecto impulsado 
por el gobierno regional que vincula a nuestra universidad con la comunidad y tiene como beneﬁciarios 
a alumnos y alumnas de liceos técnicos de esta zona del país. El recinto cuenta con laboratorios y 
salas de clases para conocer a fondo los procesos y las técnicas de la metalurgia extractiva. En 2019 se 
inauguró el túnel minero, correspondiente a la segunda fase del proyecto Escuela Mina Planta. Hacia 
el futuro queremos transformar este lugar en laboratorio para promover investigación aplicada que 
permita testear soluciones innovadoras relevantes para la industria minera.
_ The Faculty of Engineering and Sciences oﬀers a natural and technically adequate space for students 
who study mining careers to carry out their training practices, and provides the necessary tools for their 
professional development. The Mina Planta School is located in the mining district of Chancón, Libertador 
Bernardo O’Higgins region, and is a project promoted by the regional government that connects our 
university with the community and whose beneﬁciaries are students of technical schools of this area 
of the country. The enclosure has laboratories and classrooms in which the processes and techniques 
of extractive metallurgy are explained in detail. In 2019, the mining tunnel was inaugurated, which 
corresponds to the second phase of the Mina Planta School project. In the next years, we will transform 
this place into a laboratory to promote applied research that enables testing of innovative solutions 
relevant to the mining industry.
